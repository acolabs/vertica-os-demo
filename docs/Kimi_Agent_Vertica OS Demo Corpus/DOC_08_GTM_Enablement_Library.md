## 5. CALL SCRIPTS LIBRARY

### 5.1 SDR Prospecting Script (Cold Outreach)

#### Framework: P-C-Q-R (Problem-Context-Question-Request)

```
OPENING (15 seconds)
"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from PORTCO_1. I know I'm calling 
out of the blue, but I wanted to reach out because we recently helped 
[Similar Company] solve [Specific Problem]. Do you have 30 seconds 
for me to explain why I thought of you?"

PROBLEM STATEMENT (30 seconds)
"We're seeing a lot of [Title]s in [Industry] struggling with 
[Specific Pain Point]. This typically shows up as:
- [Symptom 1]
- [Symptom 2]  
- [Symptom 3]

Is any of this resonating with challenges you're facing?"

CONTEXT BUILDING (45 seconds)
"The companies we work best with are typically:
- [Characteristic 1: e.g., $XM+ revenue]
- [Characteristic 2: e.g., 100+ employees]
- [Characteristic 3: e.g., Using legacy system X]

How does that compare to your situation?"

QUALIFICATION QUESTIONS (2 minutes)
1. "How are you currently handling [Problem Area]?"
2. "What prompted you to look at this now?"
3. "Who else is involved in decisions like this?"
4. "What would success look like in 12 months?"
5. "What happens if this isn't solved?"

REQUEST (30 seconds)
"Based on what you've shared, I think it makes sense for you to speak 
with one of our [Solution Experts]. They can [Specific Value Prop] 
and typically need about 20 minutes. 

Would [Day] at [Time] work, or would [Alternative] be better?"

OBJECTION HANDLING

Objection: "Send me information"
Response: "Happy to. So I send you the most relevant content, can you 
help me understand what specific aspect you're most interested in 
learning about?"

Objection: "We already have a solution"
Response: "Many of our customers were using [Competitor/Legacy] when 
we first connected. What I'm curious about - are you getting the 
[Specific Outcome] you were hoping for?"

Objection: "No budget/timing"
Response: "Totally understand. When do you typically plan budget for 
[next year/quarter]? I'd like to make sure we have a conversation 
before then so you have all the information you need."

Objection: "Not interested"
Response: "Fair enough. Before I let you go - if you could wave a 
magic wand and fix one thing about [Problem Area], what would it be?"
```

### 5.2 Discovery Call Script (AE-Led)

#### Framework: SPIN + MEDDPICC Hybrid

```
OPENING (2 minutes)
"Thanks for taking the time today, [Name]. Before we dive in, I want 
to make sure I understand what would make this a valuable use of your 
time. What were you hoping to get out of our conversation?"

[Listen, acknowledge, align agenda]

"Here's what I was thinking - I'd love to learn more about your current 
situation, share a bit about how we help companies like yours, and if 
it makes sense, we can discuss what next steps might look like. Does 
that work for you?"

SITUATION QUESTIONS (5 minutes)
1. "Can you walk me through how [Process] currently works?"
2. "What systems or tools are you using today?"
3. "How long has your team been working this way?"
4. "Who else is involved in [Process]?"

PROBLEM QUESTIONS (5 minutes)
1. "What are the biggest challenges with your current approach?"
2. "How much time is spent on [Manual Task]?"
3. "What happens when [Problem Event] occurs?"
4. "How do these challenges impact [Business Outcome]?"

IMPLICATION QUESTIONS (5 minutes)
1. "What happens if these challenges continue for another year?"
2. "How is this affecting your team's morale/retention?"
3. "What opportunities are you missing because of this?"
4. "How does this impact your ability to [Strategic Goal]?"

NEED-PAYOFF QUESTIONS (5 minutes)
1. "If you could solve this, what would that enable?"
2. "How would that impact [Metric they care about]?"
3. "What would your team be able to focus on instead?"
4. "How would that change your [Role] day-to-day?"

MEDDPICC QUALIFICATION (5 minutes)

METRICS
- "What metrics would you use to measure success?"
- "What improvement are you targeting?"

ECONOMIC BUYER
- "Who ultimately makes the decision on this?"
- "Have they been involved in evaluating solutions?"

DECISION CRITERIA
- "What factors are most important in your evaluation?"
- "How are you comparing different options?"

DECISION PROCESS
- "Walk me through how a decision like this gets made"
- "What approvals are needed?"

PAPER PROCESS
- "What does the contracting process look like?"
- "Who needs to sign off on legal/procurement?"

IDENTIFIED PAIN
- "On a scale of 1-10, how critical is solving this?"
- "What happens if you don't address it?"

CHAMPION
- "Who internally is most motivated to solve this?"
- "Can they help navigate the organization?"

COMPETITION
- "What other solutions are you considering?"
- "What about building internally?"

VALUE PROP PRESENTATION (5 minutes)
"Based on what you've shared, here's what I'm hearing:
- [Summarize key pain points]
- [Summarize desired outcomes]
- [Summarize timeline/urgency]

Companies like [Similar Customer] were in a similar situation. Here's 
what we helped them achieve:
- [Specific outcome 1 with metric]
- [Specific outcome 2 with metric]
- [Specific outcome 3 with metric]

Would it be helpful if I walked you through how we approach this?"

NEXT STEPS (3 minutes)
"Based on our conversation, I think the logical next step is [Specific 
Action - e.g., technical deep dive, stakeholder meeting, pilot proposal]. 

Does that make sense? What would need to happen on your end to make 
that possible?"

CLOSE FOR COMMITMENT
"Can we get [Specific Date] on the calendar for [Next Step]?"
```

### 5.3 Demo Script (Sales Engineer-Led)

#### Framework: Tell-Show-Tell + Story Arc

```
PRE-DEMO SETUP (5 minutes)
"Before we jump into the demo, I want to confirm what you want to see. 
From our previous conversation, you mentioned [Pain Point 1] and 
[Pain Point 2] as key priorities. 

I'm planning to show you:
1. [Feature 1] - addresses [Pain Point 1]
2. [Feature 2] - addresses [Pain Point 2]
3. [Feature 3] - related capability

Does that align with what you were hoping to see? Anything else 
we should include?"

STORY ARC OPENING (3 minutes)
"Let me set the scene. Imagine you're [Persona] at [Similar Company]. 
It's Monday morning and you're facing [Common Scenario]. 

Today, that probably means [Current Painful Process]. 

Let me show you how PORTCO_1 changes that."

DEMO FLOW (20-25 minutes)

SCENE 1: [Problem Entry Point]
- Tell: "The day starts with [Trigger Event]"
- Show: [Demonstrate entry point]
- Tell: "Notice how [Benefit] happens automatically"

SCENE 2: [Core Workflow]
- Tell: "Now let's walk through the typical workflow"
- Show: [Demonstrate key workflow]
- Tell: "What used to take [X hours] now takes [Y minutes]"

SCENE 3: [Collaboration/Integration]
- Tell: "This doesn't happen in isolation"
- Show: [Demonstrate integrations/collaboration]
- Tell: "Everyone stays in sync without extra work"

SCENE 4: [Reporting/Outcomes]
- Tell: "Finally, let's look at the outcomes"
- Show: [Demonstrate dashboards/reports]
- Tell: "You can see [Metric] improvement in real-time"

CUSTOMIZATION (5 minutes)
"How does this compare to your current process? What questions do you 
have about how this would work in your environment?"

OBJECTION HANDLING DURING DEMO

Objection: "Can it do [X]?"
Response: "Great question. [If yes] Let me show you. [If no/roadmap] 
That's on our roadmap for [Timeline]. Can you tell me more about 
how critical that is?"

Objection: "This seems complex"
Response: "I hear that. What specifically feels complex? [Address 
specific concern]. Most customers tell us their team is productive 
within [Timeframe]."

CLOSE FOR NEXT STEP
"Based on what you've seen, does this address the [Pain Points] we 
discussed? What would you need to see to feel confident moving forward?"
```

### 5.4 Negotiation/Closing Script

```
PRE-CLOSE CHECKLIST (Internal)
□ Economic buyer identified and engaged
□ Decision criteria confirmed
□ Decision process mapped
□ Competition understood
□ Champion confirmed
□ Budget confirmed
□ Timeline established

PRICING CONVERSATION
"Based on what we've discussed, I recommend our [Package] which 
includes:
- [Feature 1]
- [Feature 2]
- [Feature 3]
- [Support level]

The investment is $[Amount] per [Period].

This is based on [User/Volume] count of [Number]. Does that align 
with what you were expecting?"

OBJECTION HANDLING

Objection: "Your price is too high"
Response: "I understand price is important. Help me understand - when 
you say 'too high,' are you comparing us to [Competitor], or is there 
a budget constraint we should discuss?"

[If competitor comparison]
"Let me walk you through what you get with us that [Competitor] 
doesn't include..."

[If budget constraint]
"Let's explore some options. We could [Alternative 1] or [Alternative 2]. 
Which would work better for your situation?"

Objection: "We need to think about it"
Response: "Absolutely. What specific aspects do you need to think through? 
Is it [Option A], [Option B], or something else?"

Objection: "Need to get approval"
Response: "Makes sense. Who else needs to be involved? Can we schedule 
a call with them to address any questions directly?"

CLOSING TECHNIQUES

1. ASSUMPTIVE CLOSE
"It sounds like we're aligned. Should we target [Date] for kickoff?"

2. ALTERNATIVE CLOSE
"Would you prefer to start with [Option A] or [Option B]?"

3. SUMMARY CLOSE
"So we're agreed on [Point 1], [Point 2], and [Point 3]. The next step 
is [Action]. Does that sound right?"

4. URGENCY CLOSE
"Given your timeline to [Milestone], when would you need to make a 
decision to hit that target?"
```

### 8.3 Stage-Specific Review Questions

#### Stage 1: Qualification Review
1. What triggered this opportunity?
2. How was the prospect qualified?
3. What pain has been identified?
4. Who is the economic buyer?
5. What is the decision timeline?
6. What is the budget status?
7. What competitors are involved?

#### Stage 2: Discovery Review
1. What business outcomes is the prospect seeking?
2. What are the specific metrics for success?
3. Who are all the stakeholders?
4. What is the decision process and timeline?
5. What are the decision criteria?
6. What is the paper process?
7. Who is our champion?
8. What are the top 3 risks?

#### Stage 3: Solution Review
1. Has the solution been validated technically?
2. What is the proposed implementation timeline?
3. Has pricing been discussed?
4. What is the stakeholder sentiment?
5. What objections remain?
6. What is the next step and date?

#### Stage 4: Proposal Review
1. Has the proposal been delivered?
2. What was the prospect's reaction?
3. Are there any pricing concerns?
4. What is the legal/procurement status?
5. What could derail this deal?
6. What is our competitive position?

#### Stage 5: Negotiation Review
1. What are the outstanding issues?
2. What is our walk-away point?
3. What concessions are we willing to make?
4. Who needs to approve the final terms?
5. What is the signature process?
6. What is the implementation start date?

### 8.4 Deal Review SOP

#### Step 1: Pre-Review Preparation (AE Responsibility)
1. Update CRM with latest deal information
2. Complete Deal Review Scorecard
3. Prepare 2-minute deal summary
4. Identify specific help needed

#### Step 2: Review Facilitation (Manager Responsibility)
1. Set agenda (5 deals x 10 minutes each)
2. Start with AE summary (2 minutes)
3. Ask clarifying questions (3 minutes)
4. Identify risks and mitigations (3 minutes)
5. Confirm next steps (2 minutes)

#### Step 3: Post-Review Actions
1. Update CRM with action items
2. Schedule follow-up if needed
3. Escalate to CRO_X if red flags
4. Document learnings for team
