                    COMMISSION PLAN DESIGN PRINCIPLES (FACTS)
================================================================================

Source: https://www.verticacp.com/blog-posts/commission-plans-for-saas-account-executives
Author: Jesse Javna (Principal at Vertica Capital Partners)

THE THREE RULES OF THUMB:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. THE 50/50 SPLIT
   - OTE (On-Target Earnings) split equally between base salary and variable commission
   - Base (50%): Provides stability
   - Variable (50%): Drives performance
   - Deviations create either complacency (80/20) or burnout (20/80)

2. THE 5X RULE (QUOTA-TO-OTE RATIO)
   - Formula: Quota / OTE = 4x to 6x (ideally 5x)
   - "A 5x ratio means that the company is generating five dollars in revenue 
     for every dollar it pays the salesperson"
   - <4x: Highly motivating but strains financial model
   - >6x: Difficult to attract talent, lower commission rate

3. THE 10% STANDARD COMMISSION RATE
   - When 50/50 split + 5x ratio are combined, commission rate = 10%
   - Example: $200K OTE ($100K base/$100K variable) + $1M quota (5x) = 10% rate

KEY INSIGHTS FROM DATA ANALYSIS:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- "The correlation between the quota level and the percentage of quota achieved 
  is surprisingly weak"
- "The Quota-to-OTE ratio is the single most indicative factor correlated with 
  sales success"
- AEs with <4x ratio achieve ~100% attainment on average
- AEs with 4x-6x ratio achieve ~87.6% attainment
- AEs with >6x ratio achieve ~73.7% attainment
- "Higher commissions drive higher performance"

ADDITIONAL COMPENSATION ELEMENTS:
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
| Element                  | Recommendation                                  |
|--------------------------|--------------------------------------------------|
| Accelerators (Kickers)   | 15% on every deal after 100% quota (example)    |
| Ramping Period           | 3-9 months depending on sales cycle             |
| Multi-Year Incentives    | Extra 2% commission on total contract value     |
| Clawbacks                | Generally discouraged as demotivating           |
| Quota Attainment Target  | 60-70% of team should achieve 100%              |

                              INFERENCES
================================================================================

The following are INFERENCES based on collected evidence:

1. VERTICA'S OPERATING MODEL
   INFERENCE: Vertica follows a "value creation" private equity model (vs. 
   passive financial sponsor), with heavy emphasis on operational improvements 
   rather than financial engineering.

   Evidence: Explicit statements about "active support," "inside sales best 
   practices," operator network, and Success Cadence promotion.

2. SALES EXCELLENCE AS CORE COMPETENCY
   INFERENCE: Vertica views sales organization design and compensation as a 
   primary value creation lever, not just a portfolio company responsibility.

   Evidence: Dedicated blog posts on commission plans, Tom Schodorf's involvement, 
   "5x sales and marketing returns" claim, explicit mention of "inside sales" 
   as a core offering.

3. CADENCE/RHYTHM AS OPERATING PHILOSOPHY
   INFERENCE: Vertica believes in structured operating cadences (daily/weekly/
   monthly/quarterly rhythms) as the foundation of rapid growth.

   Evidence: Success Cadence promotion, emphasis on "operational cadence," 
   calendar-driven management approach.

4. TALENT-DRIVEN INVESTMENT THESIS
   INFERENCE: Vertica likely evaluates investments partially based on ability 
   to attract and retain sales talent, and may help portfolio companies upgrade 
   sales leadership.

   Evidence: "Culture starts with hiring," "right people in right positions," 
   "destination employer for high-potential contributors."

5. DATA-DRIVEN APPROACH TO SALES
   INFERENCE: Vertica uses quantitative analysis (like the quota-to-OTE 
   correlation data) to guide portfolio company decisions.

   Evidence: Detailed data analysis in commission plan blog, "data-driven 
   approach to leadership" in Success Cadence.

6. CONCENTRATED PORTFOLIO STRATEGY
   INFERENCE: Vertica likely maintains a concentrated portfolio (fewer companies, 
   more attention per company) given their small fund size and hands-on approach.

   Evidence: $205M fund with $10-100M check sizes implies 2-20 companies max; 
   "active support" positioning suggests high engagement per company.
